The essay examines the role of nudging and choice architecture in big-data environments, with a particular focus on the first-mile and last-mile problems of implementing behavioral interventions. It distinguishes between traditional nudges, which operate through interface or environmental design, and hypernudges, which function at the algorithmic level through data-driven personalization and recommender systems.

The paper argues that effective behavioral change requires integrating behavioral design with predictive analytics, while also addressing the challenges of translating predictions into real-world actions (last-mile problem) and selecting appropriate and ethically responsible data (first-mile problem).
